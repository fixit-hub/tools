Frequently Asked Questions

How does the PDF to JPG conversion work?

Our converter uses PDF.js, a JavaScript library that renders PDF documents directly in your browser. When you select a PDF file, the tool processes each page and converts it to a JPG image without uploading your file to any server. This ensures your documents remain private and secure.

Is there a limit to the file size I can convert?

Since the conversion happens in your browser, the main limitation is your device's memory. Very large PDF files (hundreds of pages) might cause performance issues on some devices. For best results, we recommend converting files under 100 pages at a time.

Are my PDF files uploaded to your servers?

No, your files never leave your computer. All processing happens locally in your browser, which means your documents remain completely private and secure.

Can I adjust the quality of the output JPG images?

Yes, the converter allows you to adjust the quality and resolution of the output images. After selecting your file, you'll see options to set the DPI (dots per inch) and compression quality before conversion.

What browsers are supported?

Our converter works on all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, and Opera. For best performance, we recommend using the latest version of your browser.

Is this service really free?

Yes, our PDF to JPG converter is completely free to use with no hidden charges. We support the service through non-intrusive advertisements.
