Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our PDF to Word converter tool.

How do I convert a PDF to Word?

Converting a PDF to Word is simple with our tool:

  1. Click the "Select PDF File" button or drag and drop your PDF into the designated area
  2. Wait for the file to upload (this usually takes just a few seconds)
  3. Click the "Convert to Word" button
  4. Download your converted Word document when the process completes
What types of PDFs can be converted?

Our converter works with most PDF files, including:

  • Text-based PDFs (best results)
  • Scanned PDFs (OCR capability may be needed for editable text)
  • PDFs with images and tables
  • Password-protected PDFs (if you provide the password)

Note that complex PDFs with advanced layouts may require some manual formatting adjustments after conversion.

Is there a limit to how many files I can convert?

No, there are no limits on the number of files you can convert. You can use our service as much as you need, completely free of charge.

What Word formats do you support?

Our converter creates documents in the following formats:

  • DOC (Microsoft Word 97-2003)
  • DOCX (Microsoft Word 2007 and later)

The default output format is DOCX, which is compatible with all modern versions of Microsoft Word and most alternative word processors.

Can I convert Word back to PDF?

Yes! While our primary tool converts PDF to Word, we also offer a Word to PDF converter that works similarly. You can easily convert your edited Word documents back to PDF format when you're done making changes.
